    What the logit lens is (and why it works at all)

    The logit lens is a trick: take the residual stream at any intermediate layer, project it through the final unembedding matrix, and see which token would be the top prediction if the model stopped computing there. Early layers produce garbage ("the", "a", random filler). Middle layers start converging on the correct semantic category. Late layers lock in the final answer. The fact that this works at all is deep — it means the residual stream stays "in the same language" as the final output throughout the network, which is why adding across layers is a coherent operation. Technique due to Nostalgebraist, 2020. Model here is Xenova/distilgpt2, ~85MB quantised, cached in your browser after first load.
