How this demo works

The surface is a sum of Gaussian bumps and wells, a crude 2D stand-in for the millions-of-dimensions loss surface of a real model. At each step we estimate the gradient with a finite-difference ([f(x+ε) − f(x)] / ε), then apply x ← x − lr · ∇f. That's the literal equation of gradient descent. The chart below tracks loss vs step count, the curve you actually see in training runs.
