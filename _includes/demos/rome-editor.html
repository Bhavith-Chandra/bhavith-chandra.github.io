What ROME actually does: the model encodes facts as (key, value) pairs in mid-layer MLPs. ROME locates the layer where the subject's representation peaks, then computes a rank-one update to W_out that swaps the value while preserving the key. The edit propagates to paraphrases of the same fact (the consistency check) without destroying unrelated knowledge. Numbers shown are illustrative; real ROME edits typically achieve 95%+ post-edit probability.
