Why this tokenizer isn't a simple whitespace split

This demo uses real Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE), the same algorithm GPT-2 and (close relatives of) GPT-3/4 use. BPE starts from single bytes and learns which byte-pairs to merge based on frequency in the training corpus. The result is a vocabulary of ~50,000 subword pieces: common whole words ("the", "and"), common word-chunks ("ing", "tion"), and everything else falls back to individual bytes. That's why "unhappily" becomes three pieces but "cat" stays whole, "cat" was common enough in the training text to earn its own merge. We run this via the gpt-tokenizer library, fetched lazily. Fallback to an illustrative split if the CDN is unreachable.
